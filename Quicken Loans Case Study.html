Black Street Marketing - Marketing Campaign & Strategy

Quicken Loans, a leading mortgage lender, approached us with a challenge: they needed to generate 500 in-person attendees for a First Time Home Buyers Event in Seattle. As experts in marketing on Facebook, we knew we could help. We designed a Facebook marketing campaign that included targeted ads, organic content, landing pages, and email marketing to promote the event and drive ticket sales.

Introduction. Challenge. Solution. Results.

The main challenge we faced in marketing the event on Facebook was finding the right audience. We knew that the target audience for the event was first-time home buyers in Seattle, but we needed to narrow down our target further to ensure we were reaching the most qualified leads. Additionally, we had to create effective ads and landing pages that would drive engagement and conversions while managing the ad spend to ensure that we stayed within budget.

We used a number of ways to overcome these obstacles. To guarantee we were reaching the most suitable visitors, we first developed an unique audience on Facebook based on reliable demographic and behavioral data, including income, geography, and interests. We also produced stimulating ads and landing pages emphasizing the benefits of attending the event, including access to leading experts and free information for first-time home buyers. We used A/B testing to improve our advertising and landing pages for maximum engagement and conversions in order to properly manage ad expenditure. We also actively monitored the campaign to ensure that we stayed within budget while maximizing outcomes.

We put our strategies into action by producing a series of targeted Facebook ads with eye-catching graphics and intriguing language. To augment the advertising and increase interaction, we also developed organic content such as blog entries and social media updates. Finally, we created unique landing pages that were conversion-optimized and used email marketing to nurture leads and keep them informed about the event.

Our campaign was a success. We spent a total of $15,000 on ad spend and generated 412 in-person attendees for the event. Of those attendees, Quicken Loans processed 51 applications, which represents a conversion rate of 12.4%. This exceeded our initial goal of 500 attendees and helped Quicken Loans establish themselves as a valuable resource for first-time home buyers in Seattle.

Conclusion.

By leveraging our expertise in marketing on Facebook, we were able to help Quicken Loans overcome their challenge of generating 500 in-person attendees for a First Time Home Buyers Event in Seattle. Our targeted ads, organic content, landing pages, and email marketing strategies helped drive ticket sales and establish Quicken Loans as a trusted resource for first-time home buyers.
